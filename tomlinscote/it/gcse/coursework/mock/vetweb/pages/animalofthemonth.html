Cats

Cats have been kept as pets for about 5,000 years. The domesticated cat is believed to have descended from the African Wild Cat.
The ancient Egyptians were the first to keep cats and they had become sacred by 1,000 B.C.
Different breeds of cat have been developed. The Persian cat has a long coat. The Siamese is creamy coloured with dark paws, ears, tail and face.

What will it cost?

If you want a pedigree cat expect to pay up to £250. If you just want a friendly cat then a kitten can be adopted from an animal shelter.

It costs an average of £15 per month per cat for food and litter.
Routine veterinary care, for example, boosters and vaccinations, for a normal healthy cat costs at least £35 per year.
Pet insurance costs about £100 per year.
A litter tray and other essentials cost about £30.
Catteries charge at least £6 per day to look after your cat when you go away.

Think very carefully about getting a kitten. A pretty, lively kitten is cute and cuddly but will grow into an adult cat very quickly. Cats live for about 14 years. Once you have your kitten it is your responsibility for life.

Healthcare

Feline Enteritis is a rapidly fatal disease of cats.
Feline Leukaemia is another common, fatal disease of cats. It may affect cats of any age. If you adopt a stray kitten or cat you should have a blood test done to check for this disease. Cat Flu is another common disease of cats, though less serious than the other two.
A combined vaccine is now available for all three diseases. Please contact the surgery for more information on vaccination options. Booster vaccinations are needed every year.
Both male and female cats can be neutered at six months or older.
A neutered male should not spray around the house and he will be less likely to wander off or fight with other cats.
A neutered female won't have kittens.
Fleas
Even the cleanest, best kept cats can pick up fleas when they go outside. Fleas don't stay on the cat so you need to treat the cat and the house to get rid of them.

Toilet training

Cats are naturally clean animals. Provide a litter tray, filled with cat litter. Keep it in the same place, and make sure it is cleaned regularly. Take a new kitten to the tray after meals and on waking up from sleep.

Toys and entertainment

Cats need exercise so provide toys the cat can chase. A scratching post is a good investment to help your cat to keep his claws sharpened. If there is nothing else it will use the furniture.

Night time

Keep your cat inside at night, he will be safer and less likely to be involved in an accident.
